# Bonuses

The core principle:

> "A single offer is less valuable than the same offer broken into its component parts and stacked as bonuses."

That's why every infomercial ends with "But wait, there's more!" — adding components, even ones that were already going to be there, increases perceived value.

**Pro tip:** Never discount the main offer. It teaches customers your prices are negotiable. Add bonuses instead.

## When to Reveal Bonuses

**1-on-1 or group selling sequence:**
1. Present the core offer
2. Ask for the sale *first*
3. If YES → reveal additional bonuses (this creates a wow experience: "they were going to give me this anyway!")
4. If NO → present a bonus that *specifically matches* the perceived obstacle, then ask again

Hormozi's pattern: **"Agree, add bonus, ask if 'Fair enough.'"** Don't argue with objections — match them with a bonus.

## The 11 Bonus Bullets (the canonical rules)

For every bonus you offer, do this:

1. **Always offer them.** Bonuses are not optional. They're how you stack value beyond the core.
2. **Give each a special name with a benefit in the title.** Not "Email Templates" — "The Sold-In-60-Seconds Email Swipe File."
3. **Tell them four things about it:**
   - (a) how it relates to their issue
   - (b) what it is
   - (c) how you discovered or created it
   - (d) how it will specifically improve their lives (faster, easier, less effort — speak the Value Equation)
4. **Provide proof.** A statistic, a past client result, a personal experience.
5. **Paint a vivid mental image of life *assuming they've already used it*.** "Imagine waking up on Monday and seeing 3 new leads in your inbox..."
6. **Always ascribe a price tag and justify it.** "$2,000 value" with no reasoning is just a number. Show the math.
7. **Tools and checklists beat additional trainings.** A 5-minute checklist they can use today feels more valuable than a 10-hour course. Lower effort, faster time-to-result = higher Value Equation score.
8. **Each bonus should address a specific obstacle in the prospect's mind.** Identify the limiting belief; offer the bonus that disproves it.
9. **Solve the next logical problem they'll encounter — before they encounter it.** "After you make your first sale, you'll need to deliver — so I'm also including the Fulfillment Playbook."
10. **The total value of the bonuses should *eclipse* the value of the core offer.** Stack until the bonuses alone justify the price.
11. **Stack scarcity and urgency on the bonuses themselves**, not just the core.

## Scarcity + Urgency Stacked on Bonuses

**Bonuses with scarcity:**
- "Only people who sign up for XYZ program will have access to my Bonus #1, 2, and 3 that are never for sale or available anywhere else."
- "I have 3 tickets left to my $5,000 virtual event; if you buy this program you can get one of the last 3 tickets as a bonus."

**Bonuses with urgency:**
- "If you buy today, I will add in XYZ bonus that normally costs $1,000, for free."
- "This bonus is part of the package until Friday."

## Advanced — Other People's Products (OPP)

Get adjacent (non-competing) businesses to give their products/services as part of your bonus stack, in exchange for free exposure to your customer base. You stack value at zero cost to yourself.

**Optionally,** negotiate a referral commission back to yourself — every bonus becomes a revenue stream.

**Hormozi's pain-clinic example:** On a $400 core offer, the OPP bonuses can exceed the core price:

| OPP Bonus | Value |
|---|---|
| Chiropractor adjustments package | $100 |
| Low-inflammation food discounts (local provider) | $50 |
| Brace/orthotic discounts | $150 |
| Local health-club personal training session | $100 |
| Pharmacy savings card | $100/mo |
| **Total bonus value** | **$500+/mo** |

These cost the offering business zero. The partner businesses get a stream of qualified, pre-warmed customers. Everyone wins.

## Pulling Bonuses From the Core Offer

Question: "Should this be part of the core or a bonus?"

Hormozi's answer: **Wow Factor.** Pull out the most distinct items that can almost stand on their own — especially short-but-high-value items like checklists, infographics, swipe files, scripts, templates.

These are perfect bonuses because:
- They're easy to give away (low fulfillment cost)
- They feel substantial (high perceived value)
- They have clear, nameable benefits

## Building the Bonus Inventory

Hormozi's actionable rules:

1. **Create checklists, tools, swipe files, scripts, templates** — anything that's time-consuming to create but easy to give away. Every one becomes a future bonus.
2. **Record every workshop, webinar, event, interview.** Keep them as additional bonus assets. Past content becomes future stack components.
3. **Negotiate group discounts and referral commissions with adjacent businesses.** Build your OPP bonus inventory before you need it.

## Designing a Bonus Stack — Worked Format

For each bonus in the artifact, capture:

| Field | Example |
|---|---|
| MAGIC Name | "The 60-Second Email Swipe File" |
| What it is | 20 cold-email templates that have generated $2M+ in tracked sales |
| Obstacle it solves | "I don't know what to write in my outreach" |
| Value | $497 (similar template packs sell for this) |
| Scarcity/Urgency tag | "Only available with this offer; never sold separately" |

Repeat 4-8 times. Total values should add up to *more* than the core offer's price tag, ideally eclipsing the core value itself.
