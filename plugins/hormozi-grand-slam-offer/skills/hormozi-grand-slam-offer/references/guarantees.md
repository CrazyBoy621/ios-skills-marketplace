# Guarantees

> "The single greatest objection for any product or service being sold is… risk."

Jason Fladlien (cited by Hormozi): conversion can 2-4x just by changing the *quality* of the guarantee. Most guarantees in the wild are weak. Make yours strong.

## The Universal Formula

Every guarantee with "teeth" follows this shape:

> **"If you don't [achieve X] in [Y time], we will [Z]."**

Without the "or what" (the Z), the guarantee sounds weak — it's not really a guarantee, just a claim.

- **Weak:** "We will get you 20 clients guaranteed."
- **Strong:** "You will get 20 clients in your first 30 days, or we give you your money back + your advertising dollars spent with us."

The Z is what makes the prospect believe the X.

## The Four Types

### 1. Unconditional Guarantees

"Basically a trial — they pay first, then see if they like it." Strongest psychologically because the buyer has total downside protection. Highest refund rates because no qualifications block them.

**Best for:** lower-ticket B2C, high-margin products, anything where customer experience determines satisfaction.

**Sub-types Hormozi names:**

- **"No Questions Asked" Refund** — full refund, no friction. Variants: 50% refund, refund of ad spend, pay for competitor instead, money back + extra $1,000.
- **Satisfaction-Based Refund** — refund at any time for any reason. Hormozi used this on weight-loss; only 2 of 4,000 sales took it. Most people don't refund things they paid for, especially if it's working.

### 2. Conditional Guarantees

Include "terms and conditions." Hormozi *prefers these* because they let you tie the guarantee to actions linked to success.

> "In a perfect world, 100% of your customers would qualify for a conditional guarantee, but will have achieved their result, and therefore will not want to take it."

The conditions should be things the customer must do to succeed anyway — show up to coaching calls, submit homework, log workouts. If they hit the conditions, they hit the result; if they don't hit the conditions, they don't qualify for the refund (and it's fair).

**Conditional guarantee sub-types from the book:**

| Name | What the client gets | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Outsized Refund** | Double or triple money back, or a $X,XXX cash payout | High-margin items with consumption conditions. (Fladlien's "I'll buy your store for $25k" is the canonical example.) |
| **Service Guarantee** | You keep working free until X is achieved | Hormozi's "personal favorite of all time." Eliminates the time element; you're never at risk of losing the cash because you keep delivering until results land. Pair with action conditions. |
| **Modified Service** | Extra Y-long period of service free | Less open-ended Service Guarantee. |
| **Credit-Based** | Refund in credit toward any service | Best during upsells. |
| **Personal Service** | You work 1-on-1 with them free until objective hit | Powerful as you scale and become edified. Add heavy contingencies. |
| **Hotel + Airfare Perks** | Reimburse product + travel costs | Workshops, in-person events. |
| **Wage-Payment** | Pay their hourly rate if they don't find the call valuable | Sales calls — virtually nobody will claim it; makes the call feel risk-free. |
| **Release of Service** | Out of contract free | Businesses with enforceable contracts. |
| **Delayed Second Payment** | Don't bill again until first outcome (first 5 lbs lost, first sale, etc.) | When you have a systematized first-result process. |
| **First Outcome** | Pay their ancillary costs (ad spend, hotel) until first outcome | Same situation, different cost. |

### 3. Anti-Guarantees

Explicitly state "all sales are final." Sounds counterintuitive — but it works when paired with a credible *reason why*.

**Best for:**
- Consumables (already used; can't return)
- Items that massively diminish in value once seen ("we're showing you our proprietary process")
- Extremely high-ticket / heavily customized work
- High-touch services where refunds would be ruinous

**Example reasoning:** *"We're showing you our proprietary lead-gen funnels — once you've seen them, the value is in your hands. As a result, all sales are final."*

**Bonus framing:** *"If you're the type of customer who needs a guarantee before taking a jump, then you are not the type of person we want to work with."* This anti-pattern actually attracts better clients.

### 4. Implied Guarantees (Performance-Based)

You only get paid if you deliver. Forms:

- **Performance:** $XXX per sale / $XXX per show / $XX per lb lost
- **Revshare:** 10% top-line revenue, 20% profit share, 25% of revenue growth from baseline
- **Profit-Share:** X% of profit or gross profit
- **Ratchets:** 10% if over X, 20% if over Y, 30% if over Z
- **Bonuses/Triggers:** I get $X when Y occurs

**Best for:** B2B services where outcomes are measurable, agencies, fractional services, consulting.

The implied guarantee is the strongest form of risk reversal: "If I don't perform, I literally don't get paid."

## The Math of Crazy Guarantees

If a stronger guarantee makes you close 30% more people but doubles refunds (5% → 10%):

- Old: 100 sales × 95% kept = 95 net
- New: 130 sales × 90% kept = 117 net
- **Result: 1.23x more revenue**

Stronger guarantee → more revenue, even with more refunds.

## Stacking Guarantees

You can layer multiple guarantees:

- Unconditional 30-day no-questions-asked **+** Conditional 90-day triple-your-money-back
- Two conditionals around different outcomes: "You'll make $10K by day 60 AND $30K by day 90 as long as you do 1, 2, 3."

Stacking makes the guarantee feel ironclad.

## Naming the Guarantee

Generic guarantees feel cheap. Name yours.

- Bad: "30 Day Money Back Satisfaction Guarantee"
- Good: "Shark Tank Guarantee — in 30 days, if you wouldn't jump into shark-infested waters to get our product back, we'll return every dollar."
- Great: "Club a Baby Seal Guarantee — after 30 days, if you wouldn't club a baby seal to stay on as a customer, you don't have to pay a penny."

The name should be evocative, memorable, and a little funny.

## Jason Fladlien's Webinar Wording (verbatim)

For one-call closing or webinars, this script is the gold standard Hormozi quotes:

> "I'm not asking you to decide yes or no today… I'm asking you to make a fully informed decision… get on the inside and see if everything we say on this webinar is true and valuable to you. Then, if it is, that's when you decide to keep it. If it's not for you, no hard feelings… whether it's 29 min or 29 days from now…if you ain't happy, I ain't happy. For any reason whatsoever, if you want your money back you can get it because I only want to keep your money if you're happy."

This works because it reframes the buy decision as a "try" decision — and that lowers the prospect's perceived risk to near zero.

## Warnings

- **Customers who buy because of a guarantee can become poor clients.** They came for the safety net, not the outcome. Counter by tying conditions to actions linked to their success.
- **Higher-cost services need conditional or anti- guarantees.** If fulfillment is expensive and you offer unconditional, you eat both refund and fulfillment cost. That's how you go broke.
- **The guarantee must be credible.** Wild claims with no Z destroy trust. Make the X reasonable and the Z generous.

## Hormozi's Default Advice

Start with **service-based** guarantees ("We work for free until you hit X") or **performance partnerships**. These commit you to results and weed out clients who can't or won't do the work. As you scale and your reputation strengthens, you can move to less restrictive guarantees to increase volume.
