# MAGIC Naming Formula

> "Implicit-egotism effect: we are generally drawn to the things and people that most resemble us."

Naming sits at the end of the offer-building process because over time, *every* offer fatigues. MAGIC lets you change the wrapping paper (offer name) without changing the actual deliverables. A fatigued offer can be relaunched fresh by just renaming.

## The Formula

**M-A-G-I-C** — five components. Not all five are mandatory; **typically use 3 to 5** per name. Shorter and punchier beats longer.

You can do them in any order. Aim for a name that reads naturally — not a robotic concatenation.

### M — Make a Magnetic Reason Why

Tells people *why* you're running this promotion. (Concept: Attention.) The reason doesn't need to be deep — "so long as you believe it." Should answer either:
- *Why are they making this great offer?*
- *Why should I respond? What's in it for me?*

Examples:
- Free, 88% off, Giveaway
- Spring, Summer, Back to School, Halloween, New Year, Anniversary
- Grand Opening, New Management, New Building
- Limited Edition, Founders Edition

### A — Announce Your Avatar

Call out your ideal avatar specifically — who you're for and who you're not. Hyper-local beats regional. (Concept: Discrimination.)

Examples:
- "Bee Cave Dentists" (not "Texas dentists")
- "Rolling Hills Moms"
- "Brick & Mortar Businesses"
- "Salon Owners"
- "Retired Athletes"
- "Brooklyn Busy Executives"

The more specific the avatar, the more the reader feels "this is for me."

### G — Give Them a Goal

Articulate the dream outcome — a single word or short phrase. Can be an event, feeling, experience, or outcome. (Concept: Purpose.)

Examples:
- Pain Free, Celebrity Smile, Perfect Smile
- 1st Place, Never Out Of Breath, Little Black Dress
- Double Your Profit, First Client, 7-Figure, $100K
- Grand Slam Offer

### I — Indicate a Time Interval

How long the prospect should expect to wait. (Concept: Timeline.)

Examples:
- 4 Hour, 21 Day, 6 Week, 90 Day, 3 Month

**Caveat — ad platform compliance:** Many ad platforms (Facebook/Meta especially) reject quantifiable claims (income/weight) paired with a duration because it implies a guarantee. If your name pairs a hard claim with a duration, expect ad disapproval.

- Risky: "$10,000 in 10 days"
- Safer: "Make Your First Sale in 10 Days"

### C — Complete with a Container Word

Signals this is a bundled system, not a commodity. (Concept: Method.)

Examples:
- Challenge, Blueprint, Bootcamp, Intensive, Incubator
- Masterclass, Program, Detox, Experience, Summit
- Accelerator, Fast Track, Shortcut, Sprint, Launch
- Slingshot, Catapult, Explosion, System, Getaway
- Meetup, Transformation, Mastermind, Game Plan, Deep Dive
- Workshop, Comeback, Rebirth, Attack, Assault, Reset
- Solution, Hack, Cheatcode, Liftoff

The container word converts "training" or "course" into something with a shape.

## Pro Tips

**Find Time to Rhyme.** Rhymes stick. Don't force it.

**Alliteration is easier than rhyme.** Don't force this either. Examples that work:
- "Make Money Masterclass"
- "Change Your Life Challenge"
- "Big Booty Bootcamp"
- "Debt Detox"
- "Real Estate Reset"
- "Life Coach Liftoff"

**Name Sub-Items and Bonuses.** Apply MAGIC to each item in your stack and each bonus. "Email Templates" doesn't sell. "The 60-Second Email Swipe File" does.

**Shorter beats longer.** When you have a great long name and a great short name, pick the short one.

## When Offers Fatigue — the Change Order (lightest to heaviest)

When response rates drop on a campaign, change things in this order. *Don't jump to changing the offer or price first — those are last resort.*

1. **Change the creative** — images, videos, color treatments
2. **Change the body copy** — same offer, new words
3. **Change the headline** — the "wrapper" of the offer
4. **Change the goal name** — Free 6 Week *Lean* Challenge → Free 6 Week *Tone* Challenge
5. **Change the seasonal wrapper** — Holiday Hangover → New Year New You
6. **Change the duration** — 6 Week → 8 Week
7. **Change the enhancer** — the free/discount component (e.g., from "Free" to "88% off")
8. **Change the monetization structure** — price points, the series of offers — *only as a last resort*

> "Once you've monetized an offer, rarely should you change it. Just rinse and repeat over and over and over again."

This is why MAGIC matters so much. A working monetization structure with a fresh wrapper can run for years.

## Generating Names — A Workflow

When naming an offer or bonus:

1. Write the dream outcome in plain words.
2. Note the avatar and timeframe.
3. Brainstorm 3 container words that fit.
4. Mix and match — try 5-8 candidates.
5. Read each one out loud. If you wouldn't say it in conversation, cut it.
6. Pick the punchiest.

Don't aim for one "correct" name. Generate options and prefer the one that's both *specific* and *easy to say*.
