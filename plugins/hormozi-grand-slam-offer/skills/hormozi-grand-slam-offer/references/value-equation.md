# The Value Equation

The master mental model of the entire book. Every other framework feeds into this one.

## The Formula

```
                Dream Outcome  ×  Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
   Value  =  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                  Time Delay  ×  Effort & Sacrifice
```

The four variables correspond to four questions the prospect is silently asking:

| Variable | The prospect's question | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Dream Outcome | "What will I make / experience?" | Maximize |
| Perceived Likelihood of Achievement | "How will I know it's going to happen?" | Maximize |
| Time Delay | "How long until I see it?" | Minimize |
| Effort & Sacrifice | "What will I have to do?" | Minimize |

**Why it's division:** anything divided by zero approaches infinity. If you can drive time and effort to near-zero, value approaches infinity. This is why "done for you, instant" services command premium prices.

**Perception is reality.** You're not actually moving these variables in the real world — you're moving the *perceived* values. The London Underground famously increased rider satisfaction more by adding a dotted map (showing next-train timing) than by making trains faster. Same delay, perceived shorter.

## Variable 1 — Dream Outcome (maximize)

The prospect's actual desired end state. Not the deliverable; the *result.*

Common dream-outcome drivers Hormozi names (status-related, because most desires reduce to status):
- To be perceived as beautiful
- To be respected
- To be perceived as powerful
- To be loved
- To increase status

**Key insight:** When two products serve the *same* dream outcome, this lever cancels out — and the other three variables determine the winner. (E.g., fitness and liposuction both promise weight loss; lipo wins on Effort/Time, fitness loses despite being "better.")

**How to amplify in copy:** Frame the dream outcome from the *viewpoint of others*. Instead of "drive 40 yards further," write "your golf buddies' jaws will drop when your drive sails past theirs."

## Variable 2 — Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (maximize)

People pay for certainty. The plastic surgeon's 10,000th patient pays more than the first because of track record.

How to increase this:
- **Proof** — case studies, testimonials, before/after, screenshots, data
- **Track record** — "Used by 10,000+ customers" / "97% success rate"
- **Demonstrations** — show the process working
- **Guarantees** — directly transfer risk away from the buyer (covered separately)
- **Specificity** — vague claims feel less likely than specific ones. "Increase revenue" < "Add $50K/mo in revenue in 90 days"
- **Authority signals** — credentials, media mentions, endorsements
- **Mechanism** — explain *how* it works. "We do X, then Y, then Z" feels more achievable than "we do magic."

## Variable 3 — Time Delay (minimize)

Two components:

- **Long-term outcome** — what they ultimately buy (lose 30 lbs, build a business)
- **Short-term experience** — what they get *immediately* and what keeps them engaged

**Pro tip — Fast Wins:** Always build in short-term, immediate wins. They don't have to be the main outcome — they just need to confirm the prospect is on the right path and made a good decision trusting you.

**Pro tip — Fast Beats Free:** "The only thing that beats 'free' is 'fast.'" If you're competing with a free option, double down on speed. (MVD vs. DMV, FedEx vs. USPS, Spotify vs. piracy, Uber vs. walking.)

How to compress time delay:
- Provide a quick first result (Day 1 deliverable, instant access)
- Pre-built templates, scripts, swipe files (no "figure it out" period)
- Done-for-you onboarding
- Concurrent vs. sequential workflow (parallel tracks instead of waiting)

## Variable 4 — Effort & Sacrifice (minimize)

The "other costs" — tangible and intangible — that the prospect pays besides money. Time, attention, willpower, embarrassment, lifestyle changes, risk.

The Meditation vs. Xanax table (verbatim from the book), illustrating how dramatically this dominates:

| Variable | Meditation | Xanax |
|---|---|---|
| Dream Outcome (Relaxation / Decreased Anxiety) | 1 | 1 |
| Perceived Likelihood | 0 (most don't follow through) | 1 |
| Time Delay | 0.5 (long-term, some short-term) | 1 (~15 min) |
| Effort & Sacrifice | 0 (numb limbs, mental discomfort, daily time) | 1 (swallow a pill) |
| **Overall Value** | **1.5 / 4** | **4 / 4** |

"That is why Xanax is a multi-billion-dollar product while I know of almost no multi-billion-dollar meditation businesses."

How to reduce effort:
- DFY (done-for-you) > DWY (done-with-you) > DIY (do-it-yourself)
- Pre-built systems instead of "here's how"
- Concierge / white-glove
- Removal of decision fatigue (we'll choose for you)
- Pre-filled templates, defaults, presets

## Logical vs. Psychological Solutions

Pro tip from Rory Sutherland — sometimes the value lever isn't actually to move the underlying variable, but to *change the perception* of it:

| The logical fix | The psychological fix |
|---|---|
| Make trains faster | Show a dotted map so wait feels shorter |
| Make elevators faster | Add floor-to-ceiling mirrors so people distract themselves |
| Make it cheaper | Make fewer of them and raise the price so people want it more |

When you can't change the actual variable, change the perception.

## Scoring an Offer (the 0/0.5/1 method)

Hormozi grades each dimension 0, 0.5, or 1. Use this in critique mode and to compare alternatives.

- **0** — actively bad; the prospect would say "definitely no" on this dimension
- **0.5** — neutral or modest
- **1** — strong; the prospect would say "definitely yes"

The total (out of 4) gives you a rough comparison score. The best offers are 4/4 — that's the Grand Slam.

## Where Most Marketers Get It Wrong

Hormozi's "Get the Bottom to Zero" tip: most marketers obsess over the top of the equation (bigger Dream Outcome claims, more proof for Likelihood). That's easiest, so everyone does it — which means it's also where you stand out the least.

The unique value is in the bottom: **speed** and **ease**. Make it faster and easier than anyone else can, and you win — even if your Dream Outcome and Likelihood are merely on par with competitors.
